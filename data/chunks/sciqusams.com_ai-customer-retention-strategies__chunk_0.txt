The Real Cost of Poor Customer Health Visibility In the B2B industry, churn doesn’t equal losing a customer; in fact, it tends to mean long sales cycles, complex onboarding and a chain of lost downstream revenues. As stated by Gartner, “80% of future profits will come from just 20% of existing customers” – it’s clear why retention wins over acquisition. But here is the problem: most companies do not have a systematic, observable, and real time insight into their customers’ health. In other words, they can’t monitor: Product usage and engagement How many support tickets you opened Contract value against activity. Trends in sentiment or CSAT scores. This blind spot creates a reactive, fire drill instead of building proactively towards a relationship – the very idea of customer retention in a modern B2B setting, is fundamentally flawed.
