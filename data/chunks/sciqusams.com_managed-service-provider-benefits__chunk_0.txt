What is a Managed Service Provider (MSP)? A Managed Service Provider (MSP) is a third-party organization that manages a client’s IT infrastructure and end-user systems on a subscription (or pay-as-you-go) basis from a remote location. MSPs offer proactive monitoring, cyber security solutions, cloud management and IT Support to keep organizations running. When organizations outsource their IT service to an MSP, they can focus on their own competencies and still utilize IT managed solutions. Why do Businesses Require Managed Service Providers (MSPs)? Cost-Effectiveness – According to a report from Markets and Markets, the worldwide market for managed services is estimated to reach $354.8 billion by 2026 , expanding at a 7.9% compound annual growth rate (CAGR) . Businesses are estimated to save 30-40% on IT costs by outsourcing the function to a managed services company rather than having an in-house IT staff. Increased Security – Cybersecurity threats evolve quickly and in much the same way that information technology does. MSPs provide advanced threat detection, vulnerability management, and compliance solutions that lessen security breaches by as much as 50%. Scalability and Flexibility – Whether a startup or large enterprise, managed service providers provide scalability as a solution that grows alongside the business and in line with operational efficiency. 24/7 IT Support – Continuous monitoring of IT systems and instant troubleshooting reduces downtime, which enhances productivity for the business as a whole.
