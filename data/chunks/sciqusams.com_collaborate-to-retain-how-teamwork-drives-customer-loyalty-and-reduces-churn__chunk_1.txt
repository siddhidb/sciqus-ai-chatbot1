Improved Support through Collaboration Poor customer support continues to be among the biggest reasons customers choose to churn. When a customer reaches out with a concern, they expect to have their issue resolved quickly and efficiently. Unfortunately, when teams work in silos, the customer can experience miscommunication, slow response to their inquiries, and eventually will not have their question resolved– all of which frustrate customers and instigate them to look at an alternative company in the market. Collaborating will allow your customer support team, account managers, product development teams, etc. to work together collaboratively and deliver customer outcomes in an efficient and timely manner. When organizations use centralized communication programs, teams can view the customer history, any previous communication attempts, and the status of their inquiry all in real time– so no customer inquiry goes unanswered. 2. Customized Consumer Experience Consumers today expect a customized experience. They want a business to recognize their specific needs and deliver solutions that meet their needs. Coordination between sales, marketing, and customer success personnel gives the ability to give value in every single interaction with the customer.Let’s take a potential scenario: if a customer success team notices a decrease in customer engagement, they can notify the sales and marketing team to create and deliver customized campaigns to engage the customer again. By working together, teams may professionally create targeted offers or educational content and include customized check-ins to ensure the customer feels recognized and engaged. 3.
