Turning Afterthoughts Into Opportunities: Mastering Upselling and Cross-Selling in B2B
 
 
 
Upselling and cross-selling are usually pushed down to the bottom of the priority list in B2B sales environments with high stakes on the line. Most account managers tend to be hyper-focused on getting new accounts onboarded and resolving fire drills of existing accounts. The problem is ignoring how to grow and manage existing relationships through upselling and cross-selling is a silent killer of revenue.
The fact is that it costs six to seven times more to acquire a new customer than to retain and grow an existing customer relationship. Even with that logic established, there are still lots of companies that think about upselling and cross-selling afterwards in the sales process.
In this blog, we will walk through why this is a bad idea and how simply following upselling best-practice strategies for account managers will result in the potential for big growth. We will also share actionable tips on cross selling in B2B sales in addition to an overview of effective cross selling as it relates to B2B sales.

Best Practices for Upselling for Account Managers
Account managers are more than just support; they are drivers of growth. They should utilize these best practices when upselling to find the hidden opportunities for revenue and increase customer satisfaction.
Best Practices for Upselling:
Think like your customer‚Äôs roadmap:
 Understand their priorities and upcoming challenges. State your upsell as a solution, not a sale.
Use usage data: 
Spot patterns in usage. Are they exceeding their limits? Or are you offering them too many features? Data creates a story.
Bundle your products:
 Don‚Äôt just add a larger plan, bundle an upgrade with enhanced value, such as priority support or exclusive integrations.

Educate the team:
 Provide account managers with templates to develop a pitch and battle card. A confident team will become a successful team.
Keyword Insight: Cross Selling Tips
Cross-selling can feel awkward when not executed appropriately. The intention is not to increase sales but to offer your customer more solutions.
Actionable Cross Selling Tips:
Connect related products to customer pain points:
 For example, if a customer purchases Product A, consider what the logical connections would be to Product B.
Use customer success stories:
